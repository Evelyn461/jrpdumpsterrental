Frequently Asked Questions

What materials can I put in a dumpster?

You can dispose of household junk, construction debris, furniture, yard waste, and non-hazardous materials. Hazardous waste, electronics, and chemicals are prohibited by federal law.

How soon can I get a dumpster delivered?

We offer same-day or next-day delivery options, depending on availability and the size of your project.

Can I mix different materials in one dumpster?

Most general debris can be combined, but hazardous waste, paint, electronics, and chemicals must be disposed of separately. Our team can advise based on your materials.

Can the dumpster be placed on a city street in front of my business?

In most municipalities, placing a roll off dumpster bin on a public street requires a permit from the city's department of public works or a similar entity. You, as the renter, are typically responsible for securing this permit before we can schedule the delivery.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept major credit cards, debit cards, and business invoicing for contractors. Transparent pricing ensures no surprise fees.

How far in advance should I book a dumpster?

Same-day and next-day delivery are usually available, but scheduling 24–48 hours ahead guarantees preferred size availability.

Castle Shannon, PA Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

The first families settled Castle Shannon in 1786 in pursuit of farmland and timber and is widely assumed to be the Haas family. The most prominent farm was owned by David Strawbridge. Following common practices of Irish settlers—primarily from Ulster—in the region to name places and farms after former or ancestral homes in Ireland, he named it Castle Shanahan. Castleshanaghan is a townland in County Donegal, Ireland. Over time, the farm would lend its name to the area, as "Shanahan" would evolve into "Shannon". In 1872, the Pittsburgh and Castle Shannon Railroad line was completed, providing a direct link from Pittsburgh to the then-village of Castle Shannon. Development was stimulated by two years of free transportation and lumber transport given to anybody building a home.
